FAQS

Quick answers and personalized help.

National parks are protected areas designated by the government to preserve and showcase the natural beauty, wildlife, and cultural heritage of a region. They are managed for public enjoyment and environmental conservation.

Explore our "Explore Parks" section, which includes an interactive map highlighting the locations of all national parks. You can also use the search feature to find parks based on your location or specific interests.

National parks offer a wide range of activities, including hiking, camping, wildlife watching, photography, and ranger-led programs. Each park has unique features, so check the "Things to Do" section on the specific park's page for details.

Explore the "Educational Resources" section for downloadable materials, virtual tours, and educational programs designed for students and teachers. These resources aim to enhance learning about the natural and cultural wonders found in national parks.

Visit the "Plan Your Visit" section for information on park hours, entrance fees, camping options, and any special permits or reservations required. Be sure to check for any alerts or closures.
